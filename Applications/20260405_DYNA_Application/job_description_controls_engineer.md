# Dyna Robotics — Controls Engineer

## Company
Dyna Robotics makes general-purpose robots powered by a proprietary embodied AI foundation model that generalizes and self-improves across varied environments with commercial-grade performance. Founded by Lindon Gao, York Yang (sold Caper AI for $350M), and Jason Ma (ex-DeepMind). Raised $140M+ (CRV, First Round).

## Position Overview
Controls Engineer — responsible for the robot's neuromuscular system. Bridges high-level AI reasoning and low-level motor torque for semi-humanoid platforms.

## What You'll Do
- **Whole-Body Control (WBC):** Design and implement WBC frameworks for stable, high-bandwidth motion on redundant, high-DOF semi-humanoid platforms.
- **RL-to-Real Integration:** Lead deployment of learning-based controllers (RL, Imitation Learning) onto physical hardware; own the Sim-to-Real pipeline for contact-rich interactions.
- **Dynamic Characterization:** System identification and calibration for high-performance actuators and complex system dynamics.
- **High-Fidelity Simulation:** Build and optimize simulation environments (MuJoCo, Isaac, Pinocchio) to evaluate controller performance, stability margins, and failure modes.
- **Hardware-Software Co-Design:** Collaborate with hardware engineers; quantify impact of latency, sensor noise, and mechanical design on control performance.
- **Interactive Tooling:** Develop observability systems to visualize real-time control behavior for the broader AI team.

## What You'll Bring
- MS or PhD in Robotics/Controls (or equivalent hands-on experience)
- Deep understanding of rigid body kinematics, SO(3)/SE(3) spatial math, and dynamics libraries (Pinocchio, Drake, MuJoCo)
- Proven experience with RL or Imitation Learning for manipulation or locomotion; ability to wrap learned policies in robust safety layers
- Proficiency in C++ and Python for latency-sensitive, real-time workloads on edge compute
- Track record of pushing physical hardware to its limits (speed, stability, disturbance rejection)

## Bonus Points
- Hybrid Motion-Force Control (Operational Space Control, Inverse Dynamics)
- Low-level motor driver architectures and EtherCAT/CAN communication
- Publications at RSS, CoRL, or ICRA in robot learning or control
